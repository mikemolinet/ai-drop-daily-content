# India Becomes OpenAI's Fastest-Growing Market as Codex Users Jump 4x

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman met with Indian PM Narendra Modi this week, revealing that India has become their fastest-growing market globally with Codex usage exploding 4x in just two weeks.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI Goes All-In on India's AI Boom**

Sam Altman's meeting with PM Modi wasn't just diplomatic theater — it came with hard numbers showing India as OpenAI's fastest-growing market globally. [Codex usage has jumped 4x in weekly users over just two weeks](https://x.com/sama/status/2024826822060290508), suggesting Indian developers are rapidly adopting AI coding tools at unprecedented scale.

This timing isn't coincidental. India's massive developer population (4+ million engineers) combined with lower OpenAI API costs makes it a perfect storm for AI adoption. For context, India now represents one of the few markets where OpenAI is seeing explosive organic growth rather than just enterprise sales pushes. The geopolitical implications are also clear — as the US and China compete over AI dominance, India emerges as the crucial third pillar.

**Google Plays Defense Against OpenClaw Integration**

Meanwhile, Google is [restricting AI Pro/Ultra subscribers who integrate with OpenClaw](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/account-restricted-without-warning-google-ai-ultra-oauth-via-openclaw/122778), according to reports from 157+ users on their developer forums. Users are getting account restrictions without warning when connecting their Google AI subscriptions to the popular agent framework.

This looks like defensive positioning as OpenClaw becomes the de facto standard for multi-agent workflows. Google's heavy-handed approach suggests they're worried about losing API revenue to a platform that makes it easy to orchestrate multiple AI providers. For builders, this creates a compliance headache but also signals OpenClaw's growing market power.

**The Prompt Taste Revolution**

One of the most-engaged tweets this week highlighted something crucial: ["What separates you from anyone else using AI is your ability to steer the LLM and squeeze what you want from it, not what it decides to offer."](https://x.com/Hesamation/status/2025386547176956077) The thread emphasizes developing "taste" in prompting as a long-term competitive advantage.

This isn't just workflow optimization — it's about building institutional knowledge around AI interaction patterns. Companies that systematically capture and refine their prompting approaches will compound their AI effectiveness over time, while others stay stuck with generic outputs.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**Multi-Agent Quality Control**: Advanced users are discovering that [running 3-5 sub-agents to review Claude's work from completely different perspectives](https://x.com/EXM7777/status/2025654339130527775) dramatically improves output quality. The pattern: have agents roleplay as domain experts, skeptics, and end-users simultaneously.

**Cursor's "Shabbat Mode"**: The AI coding assistant [shipped a feature](https://x.com/benln/status/2025603363405013118) that presumably limits functionality during religious observance — showing how AI tools are adapting to diverse user needs beyond just technical requirements.

**Seedance 2.0 Optimization**: Video generation quality jumps significantly when you [add "cinematic" or "4K" at the end of prompts, use "slow motion" for smoother movement, and front-load the first 20-30 words with your main subject](https://x.com/alex_prompter/status/2025633828459389023).

**Medical Animation Shortcut**: You can now [create professional medical animations in 2 minutes](https://x.com/0xROAS/status/2025671055348576458) using Nano Banana Pro for 3D visualization plus Kling 2.6 for image-to-video conversion — potentially saving $10K and weeks of traditional production time.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**Context Over Control for Coding Agents**: The most effective approach for working with AI coding tools is [giving context about what you want to achieve, then letting the AI figure out how](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2024892806029082734). Micromanaging steps reduces effectiveness — describe the destination, not the route.

**Document-to-Web Pipeline**: A practical starter project for agent workflows is [having coding agents convert Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and PDF files into beautiful, interactive HTML webpages](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2025385122196062715) — low barrier to entry with immediately visible results.
