# Naval Calls It: "Software Was Eaten by AI"

The oracle of Silicon Valley just dropped the most concise summary of our moment. As GPT-5.4 wins over users with actual personality and Anthropic quietly builds an education empire, we're watching the great software rewrite happen in real time.

## 🔥 The Big Stories

**GPT-5.4's Personality Breakthrough Changes Everything**

Sam Altman's latest [tweet](https://x.com/sama/status/2030319489993298349) reveals something crucial: OpenAI finally cracked the personality problem. "We have missed the mark on model personality for awhile," he admits, but GPT-5.4 is different. Users aren't just praising its coding or knowledge work capabilities—they actually _enjoy talking to it_.

This matters more than the technical specs. The history of computing is littered with superior technologies that lost to more likable alternatives. If AI assistants become genuinely pleasant to interact with, adoption curves steepen dramatically. Watch for this to become the new competitive battleground—not just what your model can do, but how it makes users feel.

**Anthropic's Stealth Education Play**

While everyone obsesses over model releases, Anthropic quietly built something bigger: [13 free courses](https://x.com/thetripathi58/status/2033500333591380228) covering Claude, MCP, and AI agents. Most engineers don't even know these exist, according to the thread that surfaced them.

This is brilliant platform strategy disguised as education. Anthropic isn't just training users—they're training the next generation of AI builders on their stack. When developers learn agentic patterns through Claude's ecosystem, they're more likely to build on it. Microsoft did this with Visual Studio; Google did it with TensorFlow. Now Anthropic is doing it with AI agents.

**The Agentic Engineering Revolution Gets a Definition**

Simon Willison just published [the first chapter](https://x.com/simonw/status/2033313520436351327) of his "Agentic Engineering Patterns" guide, finally attempting to answer: "What is agentic engineering?" This matters because the field has been all implementation and no theory.

As agentic systems move from experiments to production, we desperately need shared vocabulary and patterns. Willison's guide could become the canonical reference—similar to what Gang of Four did for design patterns. If you're building with AI agents, this is required reading.

## 📡 Quick Hits

**B2B SaaS Reality Check:** "Starting a B2B SaaS company today is like starting a consumer social company in 2012," [tweets @shl](https://x.com/shl/status/2030970470451802319). Translation: the easy wins are gone, distribution is brutal, and you're competing with AI-native alternatives from day one.

**The $30M Solo Act:** Someone in Hampton is running a $30M revenue company with very few employees, sharing [the tools that make it possible](https://x.com/thesamparr/status/2032807013722337439). This is becoming the new normal—small teams with AI leverage delivering massive outcomes.

**AI Video Goes Mainstream:** Filmmaking workflows are genuinely becoming accessible to anyone, with creators sharing [high-quality production techniques](https://x.com/eptwts/status/2032859011742110079) that bypass traditional studios entirely. The content creation democratization is accelerating.

**Tennis-Playing Humanoids:** Researchers trained a robot to play tennis using just [5 hours of motion capture data](https://x.com/yoheinakajima/status/2033189231481516472). The implications for physical AI are staggering—complex behaviors from minimal training data.
