# Claude's 400B Model Takes Over iPhone 17 in Demos

The future of computing just got a whole lot more autonomous. While Apple quietly demos running 400-billion parameter models on next year's iPhone, Claude just launched the ability to control your entire computer — opening apps, filling spreadsheets, navigating browsers. The age of AI agents doing real work on real machines isn't coming anymore. It's here.

## 🔥 The Big Stories

**Claude Goes Full Computer Control**

Anthropic just shipped [Claude Computer Use](https://x.com/yoheinakajima/status/2036223891287498961) — their AI can now directly control your desktop, opening applications, clicking through interfaces, and completing multi-step tasks without human intervention. This isn't just another API or chat interface. We're talking about an AI that can actually use your computer the way you do.

The timing is crucial. While OpenAI acquired OpenClaw (the third-party tool that did this), Anthropic built it natively into Claude for $20/month. [As one observer noted](https://x.com/0xAbhiP/status/2036353600717463794): "Clawdbot got cease & desisted by Anthropic → rebranded to OpenClaw → OpenAI acquired it → Anthropic just shipped their version natively." That's how you play the long game.

What makes this different from previous "computer use" demos is the integration with Claude's existing reasoning capabilities. Early users report it's handling complex workflows — scanning emails, pulling metrics from Slack and Notion, dropping them into reports on autopilot. The security implications are massive, but so is the productivity potential.

**iPhone 17 Pro Runs 400B Parameter Models**

Apple's [iPhone 17 Pro was demonstrated](https://twitter.com/anemll/status/2035901335984611412) running a 400-billion parameter language model locally. Not in the cloud. Not with server assistance. On the device.

This validates Naval's bold prediction that ["AI coding agents can now deliver one-shot custom apps straight to your phone. It's the beginning of the end for the iPhone's dominance."](https://x.com/naval/status/2036285641462595898) Except Apple isn't ceding ground — they're claiming it.

The implications extend far beyond bigger models on phones. When every device can run frontier AI locally, the entire software distribution model changes. App stores become less relevant when AI can generate custom apps on demand. The question isn't whether this disrupts mobile computing — it's how fast.
