**GPT-5.5 Drops as Meta-AWS Strike Massive Compute Deal**

Friday's bringing heat with OpenAI's surprise GPT-5.5 launch and Meta's strategic move to diversify beyond NVIDIA with a massive AWS partnership.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI Ships GPT-5.5 with Agent Focus**

OpenAI quietly dropped [GPT-5.5](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/) yesterday, positioning it as their first model "built for complex tasks, tool use, and multi-step workflows." The release includes both standard and Pro versions across ChatGPT and their Codex platform. What's interesting here isn't just the model bump — it's the explicit focus on agentic workflows. OpenAI is clearly betting that the next phase of AI adoption happens through agents that can handle end-to-end business processes, not just chat completions. Early reports suggest the model performs significantly better on multi-step reasoning tasks, though we're still waiting on comprehensive benchmarks.

**Meta Diversifies Compute with Massive AWS Deal**

Meta and Amazon [announced a deal](https://twitter.com/StockSavvyShay/status/2047649526744551538) to bring "tens of millions of AWS Graviton cores" into Meta's compute infrastructure. This is a big strategic shift — Meta has been heavily NVIDIA-dependent for AI training, but this signals they're preparing for the inference era where CPU efficiency matters more than raw GPU power. The move makes sense as Meta's AI workloads shift toward serving billions of users rather than just training foundation models. It's also a hedge against GPU supply constraints and a way to optimize costs for inference-heavy workloads.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**a16z Leads Glif's Creative AI Platform** — Andreessen Horowitz [led Glif's Seed round](https://twitter.com/a16z/status/2047366048916365676), calling it a "creative super tool" that consolidates the fragmented generative AI landscape. The pitch: marketers currently juggle 6+ tools for a single video project across text, image, and audio.

**DeepSeek V4 Undercuts Everyone on Pricing** — Simon Willison [notes](https://twitter.com/simonw/status/2047556978831208667) that DeepSeek's new V4 models are "the cheapest in their categories while benchmarking close to frontier models." Both Flash and Pro variants significantly undercut OpenAI and Anthropic pricing.

**Anthropic Explains Claude Code Quality Issues** — Anthropic [published a detailed postmortem](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem) on recent Claude Code performance degradation, citing context window management issues. The transparency is refreshing in an industry that usually stays quiet about model performance problems.

**Tesla Cybercab Enters Production** — Elon Musk [announced](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/2047574971774611553) Cybercab production has started, marking Tesla's entry into the autonomous taxi market. No details yet on scale or deployment timeline.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**Enterprise AI Rollouts Getting Easier** — Sam Altman [shared](https://twitter.com/sama/status/2047395562501411058) that OpenAI partnered with NVIDIA to roll out Codex "across a whole company" successfully. They're now offering this white-glove deployment service to other enterprises. If you're building B2B AI tools, this suggests there's real demand for full-stack deployment support, not just APIs.

**Context Window Reality Check** — Developer insights suggest that while models advertise 1M+ token windows, they [start degrading performance](https://twitter.com/EXM7777/status/2047419498618343662) much earlier. Claude Code reportedly shows issues at just 20-30% of advertised capacity. Important for anyone building long-context applications to test real performance, not just theoretical limits.
