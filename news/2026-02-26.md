# Altman's India Blitz Shows OpenAI's Global Strategy

The AI race just shifted east. While everyone's watching Silicon Valley, OpenAI is quietly crushing it in India — and the numbers are wild.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI's India Explosion: 4x Growth in Two Weeks**

Sam Altman met with PM Modi yesterday, dropping some eye-popping stats: [India is now OpenAI's fastest-growing Codex market globally](https://x.com/sama/status/2024826822060290508), with weekly users up 4x in just the past two weeks. This isn't gradual adoption — it's a hockey stick moment.

Why this matters: India's 5 million developers represent the world's largest programming workforce, and they're embracing AI coding tools faster than anywhere else. The timing aligns with India's push for "Digital India 2.0" and massive government tech modernization projects. If OpenAI can lock in this market early, it's a massive moat against competitors.

Watch for: Anthropic and Google scrambling to announce India partnerships. This kind of growth doesn't happen in isolation — expect localized models, rupee pricing, and dedicated infrastructure announcements.

**Anthropic Acquires Computer-Use Startup Vercept After Meta Poaching Drama**

[Anthropic just acquired Vercept](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/25/anthropic-acquires-vercept-ai-startup-agents-computer-use-founders-investors/), an AI startup focused on computer-use capabilities, after Meta allegedly poached one of its founders. This is Anthropic's first major acquisition and signals they're serious about winning the AI agents race.

Vercept specialized in making AI agents that can actually control computers — clicking, typing, navigating interfaces. Combined with Claude's existing computer use features, this could finally deliver on the promise of AI assistants that work with your existing software stack instead of requiring new APIs.

The Meta poaching angle adds intrigue. Big Tech is clearly in full talent acquisition mode for computer-use specialists, suggesting this capability is seen as make-or-break for the next phase of AI adoption.
