# AI News Drop — Friday, April 10, 2026

Agent chaos is the story of the week — and the market is repricing accordingly.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Anthropic's "Cowork" Is Spooking Wall Street — and the Banks**

Anthropic's latest release — an agentic product called Cowork, featuring AI agents that automate legal tasks like contract review and NDA triage — has triggered a [sustained selloff in software stocks](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/09/anthropic-new-ai-agent-software-stocks-selloff.html) that shows no signs of stopping. The company's revenue run rate now tops $30B, up from $9B at end of 2025. That's not a typo. That's tripling in roughly a quarter.

Meanwhile, US regulators [summoned bank executives to discuss cyber risks](https://venturebeat.com/technology/claude-openclaw-and-the-new-reality-ai-agents-are-here-and-so-is-the-chaos) posed by Anthropic's newest model — an unusual move that signals Washington is finally treating AI agents as systemic risk, not just a productivity tool. When banks get called into meetings about a _software product_, something has shifted.

The through-line: autonomous agents doing real professional work (legal, financial) aren't theoretical anymore. Incumbents who sell point solutions into those workflows are getting discounted in real time.

* * *

**Chamath on What AI Actually Strips Companies Down To**

Chamath posted a [sharp framework](https://x.com/chamath/status/2042326050521448649) that's worth internalizing: AI reduces competitive moats to three things — Expert Knowledge (domain-specific know-how), Tribal Knowledge (the undocumented grey-area stuff), and a third element he cut off in the tweet. Classic Chamath cliff-hanger.

But the first two are enough to think about. The implication: companies that win won't be the ones with the best AI stack — they'll be the ones that successfully encode their tribal knowledge _into_ their agent harness before a competitor (or a foundation model) commoditizes it. The companies that can't articulate what they know implicitly are the most exposed. [He followed up](https://x.com/chamath/status/2042618502650200426) by noting the big risk is agents controlling you rather than the other way around.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**DESIGN.md is an underrated pattern.** [Meng To's team](https://x.com/MengTo/status/2041404491648602594) shipped DESIGN.md as a free feature — a structured design system file (color, typography, spacing) that you drop into a project so your AI coding agent has context from the start. No more re-prompting your design constraints every session. The pattern generalizes: any persistent context file (DESIGN.md, ARCH.md, PERSONAS.md) that front-loads what your agent needs to know is compounding leverage.
