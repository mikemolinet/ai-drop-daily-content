# OpenAI Inks Consulting Deals Worth Billions as AI Agents Hit Enterprise

The consulting wars just got real. OpenAI announced multiyear partnerships with McKinsey, Accenture, Boston Consulting Group, and Capgemini — a move that signals AI agents are finally ready for enterprise prime time.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI Goes All-In on Enterprise Through Consulting Giants**

This isn't just about partnerships — it's about distribution at scale. These consulting firms will help OpenAI's enterprise customers define AI strategy and deploy agents into real production workflows. The subtext? OpenAI knows that selling AI to Fortune 500 companies requires more than great technology — it requires change management, strategy consulting, and hand-holding through complex implementations.

The timing is telling. As [Anthropic's engineer warns that AI agents will "painfully" transform every computer-based job](https://www.businessinsider.com/anthropic-boris-cherny-ai-impact-computer-jobs-painful-change-2026-2), OpenAI is positioning itself as the safe, enterprise-friendly choice. McKinsey alone touches thousands of major corporations — this could be OpenAI's path to becoming the Microsoft of AI.

**Chamath's Brutal Take on AI Service Companies**

Chamath Palihapitiya delivered a cold dose of reality: most AI "companies" are actually just services that will get commoditized. His [viral thread](https://x.com/chamath/status/2025616174965522693) argues these businesses "control no capability or rails that couldn't be" replaced by better AI.

He's not wrong. As AI capabilities become commoditized, the question isn't whether your product uses AI — it's whether you're building defensible moats or just fancy wrappers around someone else's models. The survivors will own data, distribution, or unique workflows. The rest are playing a dangerous game of musical chairs.
