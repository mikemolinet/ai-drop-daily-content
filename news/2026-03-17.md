The AI infrastructure wars just shifted into overdrive as GPT-5.4's API launch proves builders are ready to bet big on the latest models.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Sam Altman celebrates GPT-5.4's breakout week**

OpenAI CEO [Sam Altman tweeted](https://x.com/sama/status/2033659889843913000) that GPT-5.4 had a "great first week" in the API, with "builders building fast." While he didn't share specific numbers, the enthusiasm suggests rapid enterprise adoption. This comes as [AI companies are aggressively hiring finance experts](https://x.com/damianplayer/status/2033599095869247996) — OpenAI recruited 100+ ex-JPMorgan and Goldman bankers for "Project Mercury" to train financial capabilities, while xAI and Anthropic are making similar moves. The message is clear: these models aren't just getting smarter, they're getting specialized for high-value verticals.

**The computer control arms race intensifies**

A [tweet from Jason](https://x.com/Jason/status/2033565608684491031) asking about Manus's computer control capabilities versus "Perplexity computer, OpenClaw and Claude Cowork" reveals how crowded this space has become. [Manus just launched "My Computer"](https://x.com/yoheinakajima/status/2033571373294174438) as a desktop feature, taking their agent platform local. Meanwhile, [OpenClaw continues its enterprise push](https://x.com/romanbuildsaas/status/2033864046663930201), with one founder claiming it "captured high-intent demand and converted it into 12 demos in 7 days" through LinkedIn automation. The rapid commoditization of computer control suggests we're approaching a tipping point where these tools become standard business infrastructure.

**NVIDIA doubles down on AI agents**

[CNN reports](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/16/tech/nvidia-jensen-huang-ai-agents) that NVIDIA announced "a slew of software and hardware updates to encourage the development of AI agents" — another signal that the chip giant sees agentic AI as the next major compute driver. This comes as [Clawchain.ai launched on the Chromia blockchain platform](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/clawchainai-launches-on-chromia-to-support-the-next-generation-of-ai-agent-systems-302715338.html), showing how AI agents are creating entirely new infrastructure categories.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**AI education gets validated**: [Ethan Mollick shared](https://x.com/emollick/status/2033773791688433708) results from a randomized controlled trial showing GPT-4o tutors raised high school test scores by 0.15 standard deviations — "equivalent to six to nine months of additional schooling." This is the kind of rigorous research the education sector needs to see.

**The job market reality check**: Multiple tweets highlight the employment shift. [One thread notes](https://x.com/aaditsh/status/2030009246025433481) that programmers are "74% exposed to AI" while "companies just stopped hiring" entry-level roles, with under-25 positions down 14% since ChatGPT launched. [Another points out](https://x.com/Hesamation/status/2033699758234091908) CS grads are "realizing their 6-figure dream is now an ordinary white collar job to babysit AI."

**Humanoid robotics breakthrough**: [Researchers trained a robot to play tennis](https://x.com/yoheinakajima/status/2033189231481516472) using just 5 hours of motion capture data, with the bot now able to sustain multi-shot rallies. The speed of learning suggests we're approaching human-level motor skill acquisition.

**Geographic distribution shift**: [A developer noted](https://x.com/thdxr/status/2033718591237849390) that many successful AI products he's hearing about "aren't based in SF" and are outperforming SF-based competitors. The talent and innovation distribution is clearly shifting.
