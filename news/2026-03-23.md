The hype cycle is officially over for AI agents — they're now table stakes. Andrej Karpathy, OpenAI co-founder and ex-Tesla AI chief, [declared Claude Skills and MCP servers](https://x.com/DataChaz/status/2036005335329300700) have moved "past the hype" into the "new baseline for building."

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**From Experimental to Essential: The Agent Infrastructure Stack**

Karpathy's endorsement of Claude Skills carries weight beyond typical influencer hot takes. As the creator of "vibe coding" and architect behind Tesla's self-driving neural nets, he's seen enough experimental tech to know when something crosses from clever demo to fundamental infrastructure. His assessment: if you're building anything serious in 2026, you need agent capabilities baked in, not bolted on.

The timing aligns with what builders are actually shipping. [Greg Isenberg noted](https://x.com/gregisenberg/status/2035878210508878318) that "every app/website becoming an app store" will trigger "the biggest wealth transfer in the history of software" — redistributing Apple and Google's 30% platform taxes across millions of individual builders. Skills and MCP servers are the technical foundation making this possible.

**GPT-5.4 API Gains Real Momentum**

Sam Altman's [celebration of 5.4's "great first week"](https://x.com/sama/status/2033659889843913000) in the API suggests OpenAI finally has a model that developers are actually adopting at scale. The 32x efficiency improvement from 5.2 to 5.4 — dropping costs to [37 cents per task](https://x.com/sama/status/2033659246542524566) — appears to have hit a pricing sweet spot for production workloads.

What's notable: this isn't consumer buzz but B2B developer adoption. The "builders building fast" comment suggests actual integration work, not just playground experiments. For context, GPT-4 API adoption was notoriously slow due to cost and latency issues. If 5.4 breaks through those barriers, it could finally deliver the developer platform revenues OpenAI needs to justify its valuation.

**The Vibe Coding Economy Gets Real Revenue**

[Levels.io revealed](https://x.com/levelsio/status/2035912856445624668) that Photo AI generates $110,000 monthly through subscriptions versus the $150 it would make from similar traffic via AdSense — a 700x revenue difference. He [confirmed building it solo](https://x.com/levelsio/status/2035917309294833795) "just me and Claude Code," providing hard numbers on the vibe coding economy's earning potential.

Meanwhile, [@zarazhangrui introduced](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2035962536474837019) "codebase to course," a Claude skill that converts any codebase into an interactive learning experience. This represents the kind of educational tooling that becomes possible when AI can understand and explain code at repository scale.

**Paul Graham dropped startup truth bombs** including advice founders consistently ignore: [launch fast, fire bad people quickly, don't treat fundraising as success](https://x.com/paulg/status/2036054489942175864).

**Palantir's CTO** argued that [AI revolutions are "tool revolutions, not concept revolutions"](https://x.com/a16z/status/2035102132060496323) — echoing how Galileo used telescopes rather than inventing them.
