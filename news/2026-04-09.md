# OpenAI Resets Usage Limits as Codex Hits 3M Weekly Users

The dev tools gold rush is officially here — and OpenAI is betting big on keeping builders hooked.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI Doubles Down on Developer Adoption**

Sam Altman [announced](https://x.com/sama/status/2041658719839383945) OpenAI is resetting Codex usage limits to celebrate hitting 3 million weekly users — and they'll do it again every million users until they reach 10 million. This isn't just a nice gesture; it's a land grab. With Anthropic's Claude aggressively courting developers and Cursor's remote coding going viral, OpenAI is using the oldest trick in the book: remove friction when you're winning. The timing matters. Developers are the new kingmakers in AI adoption, and whoever captures this cohort first gets enterprise deals later. Watch for usage metrics from competitors — this sets a new bar.

**The Context Window Arms Race Heats Up**

Greg Isenberg's [viral thread](https://x.com/gregisenberg/status/2042010418454184325) on Claude Skills crystallizes why most AI agents fail: context is everything. It's not enough to build an agent — you need to understand how models assemble context before taking action. This is becoming the defining skill for AI builders in 2026. The companies figuring out context engineering first will own the agent economy. Meanwhile, McKay Wrigley warns we're [not prepared](https://x.com/mckaywrigley/status/2041651309758275609) for "mythos-level" models going open source within 12 months. If he's right, context mastery becomes even more critical — and democratized.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**Anthropic Momentum Continues**: The consensus is forming that [Anthropic is the only player shipping](https://x.com/Prathkum/status/2042154665836793882) meaningful AI advances while OpenAI stagnates, Meta fades, and Google hype cools. DeepSeek was a "one-week game," and Apple never showed up.

**Steven Sinofsky Calls Wall Street Wrong**: The former Microsoft exec says Wall Street is [underestimating AI by 10x](https://x.com/a16z/status/2042022320815141324). His take: algorithmic thinking is "really, really hard" for most people with jobs, which explains slow enterprise adoption.

**Meta Ships Muse Spark**: Early reviews say it's [better than expected](https://x.com/vasuman/status/2042052641619111972) — sitting between Claude Sonnet 4 and Opus 4 levels. Benchmark-maxxed but solid.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**Turn Anything Into a Podcast**: Zara's [Personalized Podcast skill](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2041736869998948528) converts meeting transcripts into AI-hosted shows with RSS feeds. She's remixing meetings where AIs "eavesdrop" on conversations — clever use case for content repurposing.

**Cursor Goes Remote**: [Cursor now runs on any machine](https://x.com/benln/status/2041941901889130758) and can be controlled remotely. You can kick off agents from your phone to run on your devbox. This is the "coding from anywhere" future arriving faster than expected.
