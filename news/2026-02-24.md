# Anthropic Faces $1.5B Settlement as Vibe Coding Spreads

The AI industry's copyright chickens are coming home to roost, while coding itself gets a philosophical makeover.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Anthropic Hit with Historic $1.5B Copyright Settlement**

The AI giant just settled what's being called the largest copyright case in history, paying out $1.5 billion for training Claude on millions of authors' works without permission. The irony? Twitter is buzzing about how the same company that trained on everyone's data without asking is now upset that someone apparently trained on _their_ data without asking. This settlement could set the template for how AI companies handle training data going forward — and it won't be cheap. Expect other major players to face similar reckonings as the legal system catches up to the "move fast and break IP laws" era.

**Naval Declares "Vibe Coding" the New Product Management**

Naval's latest hot take is that coding agents like Claude Code have gotten so good that traditional product management is dead. His argument: when you can just "vibe" your way to working software by describing what you want in natural language, the bottleneck shifts from building to taste and vision. [Zara Zhang's 20-minute presentation](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2025829856865558654) on "Code as a Medium for Storytelling" captures this perfectly — she's a non-technical person who built 5 viral projects purely through "vibe coding" with AI agents. Alexis Ohanian summed it up best: "Claude just commoditized the act of building software — now the bottleneck isn't building, it's taste & vision & agency."

**Simon Willison Drops Agentic Engineering Patterns Guide**

The prolific developer just published the first two chapters of what might become the definitive guide to working with coding agents. His [Agentic Engineering Patterns](https://x.com/simonw/status/2025990408514523517) breaks down practical techniques for getting better results from Claude Code and OpenAI Codex. This isn't theoretical — it's battle-tested patterns from someone who's been pushing AI coding tools to their limits. Essential reading if you're serious about agent-assisted development.
