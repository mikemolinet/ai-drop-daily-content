### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Claude Code's Architecture Accidentally Goes Open Source**

Anthropic had an embarrassing security moment when [Claude Code's source code leaked via a map file](https://twitter.com/Fried_rice/status/2038894956459290963) accidentally published to their NPM registry. Developer communities are already dissecting the exposed architecture, revealing implementation details that were never meant to be public.

This isn't just a PR headache — it's a blueprint leak for one of the most advanced coding AI systems. The HN thread (432 points, 226 comments) shows developers finding optimization techniques and architectural patterns that could accelerate competing implementations. For Anthropic, this forces difficult decisions: do they pivot the entire Claude Code architecture, or accept that competitors now have their playbook? Either way, it's a costly mistake that highlights how one misconfigured build step can undo millions in R&D advantages.

**Yann LeCun's AMI Labs Raises $1B+ in Stealth Mode**

The Turing Award winner and former Meta chief AI scientist has [secured over $1 billion](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates) for Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, his new venture focused on what he calls "true AI reasoning." This puts LeCun in direct competition with his former colleagues at Meta and sets up an interesting dynamic where one of AI's foundational researchers is now betting against the transformer-heavy approaches dominating the field.

LeCun has been vocal about current AI limitations, particularly around reasoning and world modeling. With this war chest, AMI Labs likely focuses on alternative architectures — possibly energy-based models or hybrid symbolic-neural approaches. The timing suggests LeCun sees a window where established players are scaling existing paradigms while missing fundamental breakthroughs.
