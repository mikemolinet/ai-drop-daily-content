### 🔥 The Big Stories

**LiteLLM PyPI Attack Exposes Massive AI Infrastructure Vulnerability**

The AI community got a brutal wake-up call yesterday when [malicious versions of LiteLLM](https://github.com/BerriAI/litellm/issues/24512) — one of the most popular Python packages for managing LLM APIs — were published to PyPI. Versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 contained code that silently exfiltrated SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure credentials, Kubernetes configs, environment variables, shell history, and even crypto wallets from developer machines.

This hits particularly hard because LiteLLM is infrastructure-level code that sits at the heart of thousands of AI applications. [Andrej Karpathy's viral thread](https://x.com/karpathy/status/2036487306585268612) calling it "software horror" resonated with 135K+ developers who realized how exposed they are. The attack was sophisticated — not just grabbing obvious credential files but also targeting CI/CD secrets and SSL private keys.

What makes this scarier is how it exposes the fragile dependency chain powering AI development. As [Jim Fan noted](https://x.com/DrJimFan/status/2036494601750716711), future attacks could be even more subtle — contaminating agent skill files or Claude configurations rather than obviously stealing credentials. Every AI startup running LiteLLM in production just learned they need better supply chain security yesterday.

**OpenAI Extends Historic Funding Round to $120 Billion**

OpenAI just added another $10 billion to what was already the largest funding round in startup history, bringing the total to [$120 billion according to CFO Sarah Friar](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/openai-secures-an-extra-10-billion-in-record-funding-round-cfo-friar-says.html). The extension suggests either massive oversubscription or OpenAI preparing for something that requires even more capital than originally planned — likely compute infrastructure for AGI-scale models.

This comes as [Kleiner Perkins raised $3.5B specifically for AI-focused funds](https://news.crunchbase.com/sections/ai/), part of over $100 billion invested globally into AI startups in 2024. The money is clearly flowing, but at these valuations, the pressure to deliver revolutionary capabilities — not just incremental improvements — is immense.
