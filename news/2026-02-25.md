### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI's India Explosion Points to Global Developer Shift**

Sam Altman's visit to New Delhi wasn't just diplomatic theater — [OpenAI's Codex usage in India has grown 4x in just two weeks](https://x.com/sama/status/2024826822060290508), making it their fastest-growing market globally. This isn't consumer ChatGPT growth; this is developers building real products at scale.

The timing matters. While US and European markets show signs of AI tooling saturation, India's developer ecosystem is hitting an inflection point. The combination of massive English-speaking talent, lower infrastructure costs, and aggressive AI adoption by Indian enterprises is creating a perfect storm. Watch for OpenAI to announce India-specific developer programs and potentially local infrastructure in the coming months.

**Anthropic Doubles Down on Enterprise Tools**

Fresh off yesterday's enterprise agents announcement, [Anthropic is shipping Claude Cowork plugins](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/anthropic-links-ai-agent-with-tools-for-investment-banking-hr) for investment banking and HR workflows. This isn't just API integration — they're building industry-specific agent frameworks that understand domain context.

The strategy is clear: while OpenAI fights the consumer battle, Anthropic is becoming the enterprise AI platform. Their bet is that businesses will pay premium prices for agents that actually understand their workflows rather than generic chatbots with RAG bolted on.

**AI Accounting Hits Unicorn Status as Professional Services Transform**

[Basis just raised $100M at a $1.15B valuation](https://www.reuters.com/business/ai-accounting-startup-basis-raises-100-million-115-billion-valuation-2026-02-24/), signaling that AI is finally cracking professional services beyond legal and engineering. The accounting market is massive, compliance-heavy, and ripe for AI disruption — exactly the kind of regulated industry where specialized AI tools command premium pricing.
