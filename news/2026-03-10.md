# Nvidia's Open-Source Agent Platform Targets OpenClaw

The agent wars are heating up, and Nvidia just fired a major shot across OpenAI's bow.

## 🔥 The Big Stories

**Nvidia Announces NemoClaw Open-Source Agent Platform**

Ahead of its developer conference, [Nvidia is readying "NemoClaw"](https://www.wired.com/story/nvidia-planning-ai-agent-platform-launch-open-source/) — an open-source platform for AI agents that directly challenges OpenClaw's closed ecosystem. This isn't just another enterprise AI toolkit; it's Nvidia positioning itself as the Switzerland of agentic AI.

The timing is strategic. While OpenClaw dominates headlines with its app-building prowess, enterprises are getting nervous about vendor lock-in for mission-critical automation. Nvidia's open-source approach could be the unlock that lets companies build OpenClaw-level agents without the platform dependency. For builders, this means potentially getting enterprise-grade agent infrastructure without the enterprise-grade pricing.

Watch for the technical details at Nvidia's conference — if NemoClaw matches OpenClaw's capabilities while staying truly open, it could reshape how we think about agent development.

**Microsoft Ships Excel, Word, and PowerPoint Agents**

Microsoft just made [Copilot agents generally available](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/blog/2026/03/09/powering-frontier-transformation-with-copilot-and-agents/) across its Office suite. These aren't just chatbots in spreadsheets — they're autonomous agents that can schedule meetings, build presentations, and manipulate data across applications.

The real story here is distribution. Microsoft has 400+ million Office users who will now interact with AI agents as part of their daily workflow. That's potentially the largest agent deployment in history, happening quietly inside the tools people already use. For the agent ecosystem, this could be the moment agentic AI goes truly mainstream — not through flashy demos, but through boring productivity software that actually works.

**Yann LeCun's Stealth Startup Raises $1B Seed Round**

[Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs raised $1.03 billion](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/technology/ami-labs-yann-lecun-funding.html) in what's being called Europe's largest seed round ever. LeCun and his ex-Meta team have been building in stealth, but the funding size suggests they're working on something that competes directly with frontier models.

The European angle matters. With AI regulation tightening and US-China tensions rising, having a major AI lab based in Europe could provide regulatory and geopolitical optionality that US-based competitors lack. The $3.5B valuation puts them in unicorn territory before shipping a product — the market is betting big on LeCun's vision.
