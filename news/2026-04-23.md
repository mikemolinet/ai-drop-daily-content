The AI coding wars just got a SpaceX-sized plot twist, while OpenAI makes its biggest enterprise play yet.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**SpaceX Bets $60B on Cursor's AI Coding Future**

Elon Musk's SpaceX has [struck a deal](https://techstartups.com/2026/04/22/spacex-strikes-deal-for-60b-option-to-buy-cursor-later-this-year-or-pay-10b-for-work-together-ai-collaboration/) giving it a $60 billion option to acquire AI coding startup Cursor later this year, or pay $10 billion for a "work together" collaboration. The deal comes as two of Cursor's senior engineering leads already jumped to xAI last month, and xAI started providing Colossus compute to Cursor for model training.

This isn't just about buying distribution for xAI models — it's Musk hedging his bets on the AI coding stack. Cursor still relies heavily on Claude and GPT-4, with their own Composer model lagging behind. The $60B valuation implies SpaceX sees AI-assisted coding as mission-critical infrastructure, not just a productivity tool.

**OpenAI Launches Workspace Agents for Enterprise**

OpenAI [introduced workspace agents](https://openai.com/index/introducing-workspace-agents-in-chatgpt/) in ChatGPT, letting enterprises build AI agents that connect Gmail, Calendar, Slack, HubSpot and other business tools. Early users are building "Chief of Staff" agents that generate daily briefs on sales loops, product priorities, and risk assessments.

The move signals OpenAI's enterprise strategy shift from chat interfaces to workflow automation. But as one builder noted, "the templates are baselines, not moats" — the real differentiation will come from custom connectors and domain-specific data integration.

**OpenAI Ships ChatGPT for Clinicians**

Sam Altman [retweeted the launch](https://x.com/sama/status/2047149494274515032) of ChatGPT for Clinicians, a free specialized version designed for healthcare professionals. The timing aligns with OpenAI's broader push into regulated industries where domain expertise and safety protocols matter more than raw model performance.
