The model wars are back in full swing, and this time it's getting personal — with AI agents that can actually handle your outreach calls.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Anthropic drops Claude Opus 4.7 with serious outreach chops**

Anthropic just shipped [Claude Opus 4.7](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-7), and the early buzz suggests this isn't just another incremental update. The standout feature? It can apparently handle sales outreach "like a human would" — detecting buying signals, qualifying leads, and booking demos autonomously. One builder claims it does "a salesperson's work in minutes." But there's already drama: users are reporting the model immediately complains about prompt injection attempts, possibly from Anthropic's own safety systems. The model wars just got more interesting, especially with Sam Altman publicly praising people switching to Codex while throwing shade at potential rate limits.

**OpenAI's Codex momentum builds as big names make the switch**

Speaking of Codex — the migration wave is real. Sam Altman is [publicly celebrating](https://x.com/sama/status/2044921348540264614) the mass adoption but also warning about potential rate limiting issues. Paul Graham is retweeting updates about major Codex improvements, and even skeptics are giving it "a genuine chance." The standout feature getting attention: computer use that feels "as fast as a person" according to early users. When Naval tweets that "vibe coding is more addictive than any video game ever made," you know something's clicking with builders.

**Qwen3.6-35B-A3B goes open source with agentic coding**

China's AI scene just made a big open source play. [Qwen3.6-35B-A3B](https://qwen.ai/blog?id=qwen3.6-35b-a3b) launched with "agentic coding power" available to everyone — no API fees, no rate limits. This matters because it's specifically built for autonomous coding workflows, and being fully open means builders can fine-tune and deploy however they want. It's getting serious Hacker News traction (1,146 points) as developers realize they can run GPT-4 class coding assistance on their own infrastructure.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**NVIDIA fires 800 software engineers, replaces them with AI agents** — The irony is thick: the chip company powering the AI revolution just [replaced 800 of its own engineers](https://x.com/BowesChay/status/2045109048710754306) with AI agents. CEO Jensen Huang confirmed the agents were built by the same software engineers they're replacing. It's a stark preview of how even AI companies are reshaping their own workforces.

**Perplexity called out for undisclosed promotion campaigns** — Nikita Bier (former HQ Trivia founder) [publicly challenged Perplexity](https://x.com/nikitabier/status/2044902122995548330) to stop running undisclosed promotion campaigns, saying it "deceives users and doesn't reflect well on your integrity." The callout is getting traction from other builders who are tired of AI companies gaming social platforms.

**Android CLI promises 3x faster app development** — Google shipped an [Android CLI tool](https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2026/04/build-android-apps-3x-faster-using-any-agent.html) designed to work with any AI agent. The pitch: build Android apps 3x faster by letting agents handle the boilerplate. It's getting attention from mobile developers who've been waiting for AI tooling that actually understands Android's complexity.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**Andrew Chen's AI workflow insight** — A16z's Andrew Chen [nailed something important](https://x.com/andrewchen/status/2044936755661697531): "for workflows using AI, you spend LESS time for the middle 80% of the work — instead, you spend MORE of the saved time on the first 10% and last 10% where taste expresses itself." Worth exploring if you're building agentic workflows at scale.
