**AI Coding Agents Ship to iPhones as $11B Legal AI Deal Drops**

Mobile's about to get weird. Naval's calling it "the beginning of the end for iPhone dominance" as AI coding agents start delivering custom apps straight to phones. Meanwhile, Harvey just raised $200M at an $11B valuation, proving vertical AI is where the real money lives.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Harvey Hits $11B Valuation in Legal AI Land Grab**

Legal AI startup [Harvey raised $200 million](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-25/legal-ai-startup-harvey-raises-funds-at-11-billion-valuation) at an $11 billion valuation, making it one of the most valuable vertical AI plays to date. The company builds AI tools specifically for law firms and has been quietly signing up major clients while everyone else chases general-purpose models.

This matters because it's proof that vertical AI applications can command massive valuations even in a market obsessed with foundation models. Harvey's success suggests the real money might be in industry-specific AI that solves actual business problems, not just general intelligence. Watch for more vertical AI companies to raise at eye-watering valuations as VCs hunt for the next Harvey.

**One-Shot App Generation Hits Mobile**

[Naval's tweet](https://x.com/naval/status/2036285641462595898) about AI coding agents delivering custom apps directly to phones signals a major shift in how we think about mobile software. Instead of downloading pre-built apps from app stores, users might soon describe what they want and get a custom app generated on the fly.

The technical infrastructure for this is already emerging—we're seeing AI agents that can write, test, and deploy code autonomously. The missing piece has been mobile delivery, but if that's getting solved, it could fundamentally change the app economy. Apple and Google's app store duopoly suddenly looks a lot less permanent when users can bypass the entire distribution model.

**Sam Altman Steps Down from Helion Board Over OpenAI Conflicts**

Sam Altman [announced he's leaving](https://x.com/sama/status/2036137695605563682) the Helion board as the fusion energy company explores working with OpenAI "at significant scale." This is a big deal because it suggests OpenAI is serious about securing massive amounts of energy for future AI training runs.

Helion is one of the most promising fusion startups, and if they can deliver on their timeline, it could solve AI's energy bottleneck problem. Altman stepping down removes governance conflicts but also signals these companies are moving toward a major partnership. Energy is becoming the new semiconductor chokepoint for AI development.
