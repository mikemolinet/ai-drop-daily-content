# Block Cuts 4,000 Jobs in First AI-Driven Restructuring

Friday's looking intense — AI just claimed its first major corporate scalp, while PewDiePie somehow built a better coding model than Meta.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Block cuts half its workforce citing AI advances**

Jack Dorsey's Block just laid off 4,000 employees — roughly half the company — in what appears to be the first major AI-driven corporate restructuring. Unlike typical cost-cutting layoffs, Block explicitly cited "advances in AI" as the reason for eliminating roles across payments, merchant services, and back-office operations. This isn't speculative future-proofing; it's happening now. The move signals we've crossed from "AI will change jobs" to "AI is changing jobs today." Other fintech and payments companies are reportedly watching Block's experiment closely. If Block maintains service levels with half the headcount, expect copycats within months.

**PewDiePie's LLM outperforms Meta and OpenAI on coding benchmarks**

In the most unexpected AI story of 2026, YouTuber PewDiePie trained a custom LLM that's beating DeepSeek v2.5, LLAMA-4, and GPT-4o on coding benchmarks. While details are sparse, this highlights how accessible model training has become — and how much room for improvement exists in coding-specific models. If a content creator with zero ML pedigree can outperform billion-dollar research teams on specialized tasks, it suggests either massive untapped potential in focused fine-tuning or serious gaps in how big tech approaches coding models. Either way, it's embarrassing for the incumbents.

**Naval's "vibe coding" sparks heated technical debate**

Naval's viral thread about "vibe coding" replacing traditional programming has ignited fierce pushback from technical leaders. Karpathy responded that while AI democratizes basic coding, "deep technical expertise may be _even more_ of a multiplier than before because of the added leverage." The debate cuts to the core of where AI development is heading: toward simplified interfaces for casual users, or toward more powerful tools for experts. Both are probably true, but the implications for hiring, education, and career paths couldn't be more different.

### 📡 Quick Hits

Anthropic acquired computer-use AI startup Vercept after Meta poached one of its founders, highlighting the talent wars heating up around agentic AI capabilities. **OpenClaw** shipped major updates including external secrets management and thread-bound agents, continuing their aggressive release pace. Anthropic's CEO Dario Amodei released a statement on discussions with the Department of War — expect more AI companies to face military partnership decisions. **Claude for Open Source** launched, giving maintainers 6 months of free Claude Max 20x access.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

If you're building coding agents, focus on intent detection from natural language rather than requiring users to learn specific commands.
