### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Meta's $2B Manus AI Ships in 7 Weeks — Fastest Product Integration in Company History**

Meta just proved AI agents can compress enterprise development timelines by 10x. Their [Manus AI acquisition](https://x.com/EXM7777/status/2037274146359672989) — a $2 billion purchase — shipped inside Ads Manager in just 7 weeks, setting a new company record. Users can now type "why did my ROAS drop 18% last month?" and get AI investigations across their data plus competitor analysis from the Ad Library.

This isn't just about Meta. It's proof that AI-native acquisitions can skip the usual 18-month integration hell that kills most enterprise deals. When your acquisition target is already built for AI workflows, you're buying velocity, not just technology. Expect acquisition multiples to split into two markets: traditional companies and AI-native ones that promise Meta-speed integration.

**Naval Calls iPhone's Dominance Over as AI Agents Deliver One-Shot Apps**

Naval just [declared war on the App Store model](https://x.com/naval/status/2036285641462595898): "AI coding agents can now deliver one-shot custom apps straight to your phone. It's the beginning of the end for the iPhone's dominance." He's betting that when any user can generate a custom app in seconds, Apple's walled garden becomes a liability, not an asset.

The argument: why download TikTok when you can generate "TikTok but for my running group" in 30 seconds? Why accept Spotify's interface when you can have "music player that sorts by my energy levels"? If agents can code, compile, and deploy instantly, the whole "there's an app for that" model crumbles. Apple's response — or lack thereof — in the next six months will define the next decade of mobile computing.
