OpenAI just dropped their most capable model yet, and it's already changing how developers think about AI integration.

## 🔥 The Big Stories

**GPT-5.4 Goes Live With Game-Changing Computer Use**

OpenAI launched [GPT-5.4](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-4/) today, and early testers are calling it a watershed moment. The model ships with native computer use capabilities, 1M token context windows, and mid-response steering — meaning you can redirect the AI while it's thinking through a problem.

Matt Shumer, who's been testing it for a week, says it's "the first model that makes the 'which model should I use?' conversation feel almost over." The biggest surprise? Most users are finding they barely need Pro tier anymore. That's a significant shift in the economics of AI usage, potentially democratizing access to state-of-the-art capabilities.

The computer use feature is particularly notable — while Anthropic pioneered this with Claude, OpenAI's implementation appears more seamless. Developers are already building agents that can navigate complex desktop workflows without the usual API gymnastics.

**Karpathy's Training Speed Breakthrough**

Andrej Karpathy's [nanochat now trains GPT-2 capability models](https://twitter.com/karpathy/status/2029701092347630069) in just 2 hours on a single 8xH100 node, down from 3 hours last month. The key wasn't just optimization — switching from FineWeb-edu dataset made the biggest difference.

This matters because we're approaching "interactive" AI training speeds. When you can iterate on model capabilities in hours rather than days, the entire research feedback loop accelerates. Karpathy's also pushing a meta-benchmark: "what is the research org agent code that produces improvements on nanochat the fastest?" The race isn't just about better models — it's about better model-building systems.
