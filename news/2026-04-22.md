SpaceX Bids $60B for Cursor as AI Editor War Heats Up

The AI coding war just got a massive plot twist. SpaceX announced it has an agreement to acquire AI code editor Cursor for $60 billion — a staggering sum that would make this one of the largest tech acquisitions in history. The move signals Elon Musk's broader AI strategy, connecting his rocket company to his xAI venture through what's essentially a distribution play for AI coding tools.

**The acquisition math is wild but makes sense.** Cursor's best models are still Claude and GPT-4 — they haven't cracked the foundational model problem yet. What SpaceX is buying is pure distribution: millions of developers already hooked on Cursor's interface. The bet is that xAI's models will eventually power the experience, giving Musk's AI lab direct access to the most valuable AI use case today: code generation.

Two key Cursor engineering leads (Andrew Milich and Jason Ginsberg) already jumped to xAI last month, and xAI started renting Colossus compute to Cursor for model training just last week. This wasn't a surprise — it was a coordinated play months in the making.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**OpenAI Drops ChatGPT Images 2.0**

OpenAI quietly shipped [ChatGPT Images 2.0](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-images-2-0/) yesterday, promising better image understanding and generation capabilities. The update focuses on higher resolution processing and more accurate text recognition within images. While incremental, it's another sign that multimodal AI is moving from experimental to production-ready across consumer applications.

**Jeff Bezos Closes In on $10B AI Lab Funding**

[Project Prometheus](https://www.businessinsider.com/jeff-bezos-project-prometheus-valued-at-38-billion-2026-4), Bezos' secretive AI startup, is finalizing a $10 billion funding round that would value the company at $38 billion post-money. The lab is reportedly developing models with capabilities that go beyond current language models, though details remain scarce. The round would make it one of the largest AI investments to date, putting Bezos in direct competition with OpenAI and Anthropic.
