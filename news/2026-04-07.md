The AI tooling stack is fracturing — Claude Code is reportedly "unusable" while Anthropic's revenue growth defies gravity.

### 🔥 The Big Stories

**Claude Code Users Revolt Over February Updates**

A [GitHub issue](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/42796) with over 1,100 points and 600+ comments is documenting widespread frustration with Claude Code's recent updates. Senior engineers report the platform has become "unusable for complex engineering tasks" after February changes. This comes as Anthropic celebrates explosive revenue growth — a reminder that scaling revenue and maintaining product quality don't always move in lockstep. The timing is particularly awkward given how much of Anthropic's $30B ARR likely comes from developer-focused products.

**Freestyle Launches Sandboxes for Coding Agents**

[Freestyle](https://www.freestyle.sh/) emerged from stealth with secure sandbox environments specifically designed for AI coding agents. The [Launch HN thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=40234567) shows genuine interest from developers who've been burned by agent security issues. Smart positioning — instead of building another coding agent, they're building the infrastructure layer that makes other people's agents safer to run. This feels like the pick-and-shovels play for the agent coding boom.

**AI Singer Claims 11 iTunes Chart Spots**

An AI-generated artist called "Eddie Dalton" now occupies [eleven spots on the iTunes singles chart](https://www.showbiz411.com/2026/04/05/itunes-takeover-by-fake-ai-singer-eddie-dalton-now-occupies-eleven-spots-on-chart-despite-not-being-human-or-real-exclusive), sparking debates about platform authenticity. This isn't just a music story — it's a preview of what happens when AI content generation meets recommendation algorithms at scale. Expect similar chart manipulation across all content platforms soon.

### 📡 Quick Hits

**Enterprise AI Agents Hit 80% Resolution Rates** — New data from Automation Anywhere shows AI service agents are resolving more than 80% of employee support requests, forcing companies to [rethink SaaS pricing models](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ai-agents-force-rethink-of-saas-pricing-and-improve-customer-experiences-302734934.html). Per-seat pricing breaks when agents do the work.

**Nvidia Acquires Groq Assets** — Nvidia is reportedly acquiring key technology from Groq to integrate their LPU (Language Processing Unit) architecture for high-value agentic workloads. Another sign that the AI hardware war is shifting toward inference optimization.

**Alibaba Commits $100B to AI** — Five-year commitment signals sustained industry momentum, particularly in Asia where AI infrastructure investment is accelerating faster than regulatory frameworks.

**Q1 Funding Hits Records** — [More than 87% of Q1 investment went to AI-related companies](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/funding-surges-all-stages-ai-north-america-q1-2026/), with North American funding surging across all stages. The money is still flowing, but it's getting more concentrated.

### 💡 Builder's Corner

**The Karpathy Corpus Pattern** — Multiple tweets are highlighting Andrej Karpathy's approach: instead of RAG, put 100 articles (400K words) in one directory the LLM can read end-to-end. [@aaditsh notes](https://x.com/aaditsh/status/2041105759350755647) "he didn't get a better model, he got a better corpus." Every enterprise has a "Karpathy wiki" worth of knowledge — the trick is packaging it right.

**AI Agent Marketing Hack** — [@zarazhangrui shares](https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2040956069699297314) a clever pattern: include prompts that make your agent introduce and demo your product's features when writing documentation. Turn user agents into marketers for your tools.
